# Brainstorm Session
> Multi-department ideation protocol for generating and refining research approaches.
> Owner: Research Director | Version: 1.0

## Overview
A structured brainstorming protocol that leverages perspectives from all departments
to generate diverse approaches to a research challenge. Moves from divergent thinking
(many ideas) to convergent thinking (best ideas) to action planning (execute ideas).

**Estimated Duration:** 1 session
**Required Agents:** Research Director + all department leads

---

## Phase 1: Problem Statement
**Lead:** Research Director

### Process
1. **Define the challenge clearly**
   - State the problem in one sentence
   - Provide necessary background context (2–3 paragraphs max)
   - Specify what a successful outcome looks like

2. **Set scope boundaries**
   - What is in scope vs. out of scope
   - Time constraints (deadline, available effort)
   - Resource constraints (data availability, tool access)

3. **Identify constraints**
   - Must-have requirements (non-negotiable)
   - Nice-to-have preferences (negotiable)
   - Known blockers or risks

### Output
- Problem statement document (shared with all departments)
- Scope boundary definitions
- Constraint list

---

## Phase 2: Divergent Thinking
**Lead:** All Department Leads (parallel contributions)

### Process
Each department proposes approaches from their unique perspective.
No criticism or filtering at this stage — volume and variety are the goals.

**Research Department** (Research Scout / Research Director)
- What does the existing literature suggest?
- Are there established frameworks or theories to apply?
- What have similar studies done?
- What gaps in the literature could this address?

**Data Science Department** (Data Analyst)
- What analytical approaches could work?
- What data is available or obtainable?
- What statistical methods are appropriate?
- What visualizations would be informative?

**Writing Department** (Manuscript Writer / Technical Editor)
- How should findings be framed for the target audience?
- What narrative structure would be most compelling?
- What format best serves the content (paper, report, brief)?
- Are there communication challenges to plan for?

**QA Department** (Scientific Reviewer / Methodology Auditor)
- What risks and failure modes exist?
- Where are the methodological pitfalls?
- What could go wrong with each proposed approach?
- What validation steps are essential?

**Domain Expert**
- What domain-specific considerations apply?
- Are there field conventions that must be followed?
- What domain knowledge is essential context?
- Are there controversial aspects to navigate carefully?

### Output
- Raw idea list from each department (unfiltered)
- Minimum 3 ideas per department

---

## Phase 3: Convergent Thinking
**Lead:** Research Director

### Process
1. **Identify common themes**
   - Group similar ideas across departments
   - Note ideas that were independently proposed by multiple departments
   - Identify complementary ideas that could be combined

2. **Rank proposals**
   - Score each idea/theme on:
     - **Feasibility** (1–5): Can we actually do this with available resources?
     - **Impact** (1–5): How much does this advance the research goal?
     - **Novelty** (1–5): Does this offer a fresh perspective or approach?
   - Composite score = Feasibility × Impact (novelty as tiebreaker)

3. **Combine complementary ideas**
   - Look for ideas that address different aspects of the problem
   - Merge partial ideas into more complete approaches
   - Identify synergies between departments

4. **Select top approaches**
   - Choose 2–3 highest-ranked approaches
   - Ensure selected approaches are sufficiently different (avoid redundancy)
   - Confirm feasibility with relevant department leads

### Output
- Ranked idea list with scores
- Top 2–3 selected approaches with rationale
- Combined approach descriptions

---

## Phase 4: Action Planning
**Lead:** Research Director

### Process
1. **Assign tasks to departments**
   - Break each selected approach into concrete tasks
   - Assign each task to the most appropriate department/agent
   - Identify dependencies between tasks

2. **Define success criteria**
   - What does "done" look like for each task?
   - What quality thresholds must be met?
   - How will we know if an approach isn't working?

3. **Set checkpoints**
   - Define intermediate milestones
   - Schedule check-in points to assess progress
   - Identify decision points (go/no-go gates)
   - Plan pivot criteria (when to switch approaches)

### Output
- **Brainstorm Summary**: Problem statement + selected approaches + rationale
- **Action Plan**: Task list with assignments, dependencies, and timeline
- **Success Criteria**: Measurable outcomes for each task and overall goal
- **Checkpoint Schedule**: When to review progress and make decisions
